Sasha Originals Case Study

This is a school project in which I designed a logo for a fictional boutique that made special dresses for special little girls. The shop, Sasha Originals, had a focus on unique clothing, some with musical buttons in them.

The project focused on creation of a two-color logo that incorporated both text and graphics, as well as mock-ups for items used by the business.

Step Up Case Study

Works created in a multimedia class that are related to Step Up, a fictional health gaming app.

I generally aimed for a calm and peaceful, yet fun, feel with my works. The logo and app should feel fun and natural to users, and have a calm yet pleasing color scheme. Overall, users should feel inspired and motivated.

The logo was created from a pool of potential logos that the class created and compiled. I used a basic sketch idea from a classmate to create the logo shown. This logo was shown to be the most easily replicated from memory among my peers and I felt it conveyed the proper ideas.

The logo is meant to represent positivity, growth, and peace, as well as call to mind an ascending line chart. All other elements (colors, font, etc.) were chosen on the basis of supporting the fun, empowering, clean, and peaceful feeling that the logo should provide. Two-color printing was not a restriction on this logo.

The logo (Health esteem) at the top of the style guides is a temporary one that I created to tie the design together before the actual logo was solidified.

CTEC Video Graphics

Graphics created using Illustrator for a video project in CTEC 4350. My group created a video detailing the learning objectives for the CTEC course. We identified four main areas to cover: video, image, code, and soft or communication skills. We decided to create four mascots to represent these groups in order to make the video more interesting and fun, to tie the ideas together in the users mind, and to create further examples of the sort of work prospective students may create while studying in the CTEC major.
